Kichadi

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegan butter or neutral oil
  • ½ cup moong dal (see note for substitutions)
  • ½ cup white basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 sprig of curry leaves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 pinch hing (asafetida) – optional
  • 2 inch ginger, grated (~1 tbsp)
  • 1 green chili, minced
  • ½ red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • ½-1 tsp garam masala (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup mixed veggies (carrots, green beans, peas, bell peppers, cauliflower, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Soak the moong dal and rice in water until ready to use in the recipe.
  2. Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode over medium heat (or use a pressure cooker/stovetop pot). Add vegan butter or oil.
  3. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for 1 minute. Add hing and simmer for 30 seconds. Stir in ginger and green chilis and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add onions and cook until translucent.
  5. Add tomatoes and a big pinch of salt. Cook for 3–4 minutes until the tomato juices reduce.
  6. Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. Add the chopped vegetables and cook for a couple of minutes.
  7. Drain the soaked dal and rice. Add them to the pot along with 4 cups of water and ½ tsp salt.
  8. Cooking methods:
    • Instant Pot: Cook for 12 minutes at high pressure with natural release.
    • Pressure cooker: Start on high heat. After 1 whistle, reduce to low and cook for 5 more minutes. Let the pressure release naturally.
    • Stovetop: Bring to a boil, reduce to medium-low, and simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes until mushy and porridge-like.
  9. Taste and adjust salt if needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve warm.
